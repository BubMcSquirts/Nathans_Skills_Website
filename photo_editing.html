Photo Editing and Photography
While animation is currently only a hobby for me, photo editing is my job. I work for Solid Custom Heirlooms, a custom furniture maker based in Ohio and started by my grandfather. My job is to edit the photos that go out to the world, and can be found at our website: SolidCustomHeirlooms.com. I use Adobe Photoshop, and with it I edit endless amounts of photos, hardly any of which are high quality to begin with. On our website, there are between 8 and 12 photos in the average furniture listing, and there are over 380 listings, almost all of which I have edited since I began the job in spring 2021. However, I also do photography as a hobby. I have a Nikon D3500, and whenever I go on a trip to somewhere I know there will be good photos, I take my camera. I cannot say that I am a great photographer, but editing endless amounts of low quality photos has given me a great idea of what a good photo is not. Here are some of my hobby photos.
RC Rock Crawling: A photo of my RC car, an Axial SCX24, on a rock.
I had the opportunity to photograph this classic Schwinn bike, which we found used at a bike shop. The bike was $75, and after some upgrades we must have put around $125 total into it. Even though we put practically nothing into it ($125 is super cheap for a bike), these photos stand as good examples of how the right lighting and a bit of water from a hose can make something kind of junky look more expensive than it is.
Schwinn Traveler: I improved the effect by spraying the bike with water first. These images are lower resoloution for better use on the web.
