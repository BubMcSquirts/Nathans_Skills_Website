Video Editing

I cannot remember exactly when I began video editing, but I should say it coincided with my obsession with water bottle flipping. Naturally, such an extraordinary feat as flipping a plastic bottle in the air and landing it upright engendered in me a desire to share this achievment with the world. So I discovered iMovie, Apple's video editor that Macs come with. I used the very same camcorder that I implemented as a means of stop motion, aimed it at my subject and recorded whatever I wanted. I then imported the video into iMovie, made some cuts and exported the video. All this took place while my laptop's fans were screaming for mercy as they did their best to impersonate a rocket ship taking flight. The system worked, but it could have been better.
Then I discovered Blender, that oh so free and open source solution to all of life's creative problems. It was amazing! The people at the Blender Institute are not joking when they say that Blender can run on a potato. If I remember correctly, it ran faster than iMovie, but not fast enough for my ambitions.
After a couple of years I bought myself a powerful PC. Now Blender was ridiculously fast. But it's feature set is lacking in some ways, especially for the new user. So I researched other options, and I came upon DaVinci Resolve, and now I have access to Adobe Premiere Pro as well. These professional level video editors are great, and it is good to know how to use them, but lately I have found myself using Blender more again. Since I began with Blender they have added new features to the video editor that make it much more usable, and I am also more comfortable with the program in general than I was. But if Blender cannot handle something, I just use Adobe Premiere Pro.
