Stop-Motion Animation

Stop motion was the first form of animation that I happened upon. When I was 13 I had a cheap camcorder that I used to record various things: water bottle flipping, basketball trick shots, and other such great activities. Then I discovered that if I pressed the photo button on the camcorder while recording a video I could pause the video. If I did this, I could then move to a different spot and press the photo button again, which would resume the recording process. This looked like I had edited the video, when in reality I had just pressed a button. Then I discovered that if I pressed the photo button twice very quickly I could capture a very minuscule amount of time. I had LEGO, so I decided to take some movies of my minifigures doing various things with this method I had newly found, pressing the pause button twice very quickly, moving the LEGO man, and then doing the same thing over and over again. I had discovered stop-motion when I had no idea what it was or that it existed.
WAR! One of my first stop-motion animations. I just set up a camera and went straight ahead.
I showed my parents what I had made, and announced that I had discovered this new thing that let you make LEGO men look like they were moving! My parents told me that although I had discovered it, stop-motion was not a new thing. They told me that it was what was used to create Wallace and Gromit, which I was familiar with. Although a little crest-fallen that I was not the first to use stop-motion, I was excited about it none the less.
